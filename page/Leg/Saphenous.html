Saphenous

Indications

Lacerations, abscesses to medial leg

Contraindications

Infection overlying injection site
Allergy to local anesthetic

Equipment

5-10cc of local anesthetic of choice
22G needle
Cleansing solution
Ultrasound transducer sterile cover

Preparation

Position

Position the patient supine, elevate and externally rotate the target extremity to expose the medial aspect

Ultrasound

Landmarks

1. Place the transducer in transverse plane along the medial aspect of the lower extremity approximately 10cm proximal to the knee (medial femoral condyle)
2. The saphenous nerve is located deep to the sartorius muscle

Technique

1. Introduce needle from anterior aspect
2. In-plane needle visualization
3. Infiltrate local anesthetic around identified saphenous nerve

Source

Highland Ultrasound
